◆ Lead Story

Lilly's Zepbound Gets High-Dose Approval; Payers Brace for Budget Impact

FDA approved a higher-dose formulation of Eli Lilly's Zepbound (tirzepatide) for obesity, showing 26% average weight loss in Phase 3 trials—the highest efficacy yet demonstrated in an approved obesity drug. UnitedHealthcare and CVS Caremark both signaled they'll cover it but with prior authorization requirements.

Analyst estimates suggest that if just 10% of eligible patients get on GLP-1s, payer drug spend could increase 15-20%. The coverage decisions made in the coming months will shape whether GLP-1s become mass-market treatments or stay restricted to severe cases.

→ The So What

Watch for employer pushback on premium increases. The tension between clinical evidence (these drugs work) and economic reality (they're expensive at scale) will define healthcare economics for the next decade.
