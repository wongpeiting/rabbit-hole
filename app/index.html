What this industry is

Healthcare in the US is a $4.5 trillion system—roughly 17% of GDP[1]—spanning how drugs get made, how care gets delivered, and how it all gets paid for.

It's notoriously complex because incentives are misaligned: the people receiving care aren't the ones paying for it, and the people paying for it aren't the ones deciding what care to deliver.

The five sectors you need to know

Sector What they do Key Players Revenue Model
Pharma & Biotech Discover and manufacture drugs Pfizer, Merck, J&J, Lilly, Moderna, Gilead Patent-protected pricing → PBM negotiations → insurance reimbursement
Payers Insurance companies that pay for care UnitedHealth, Elevance, CVS/Aetna, Cigna, Humana Premiums from employers/individuals/government; profit = premiums minus claims
Providers Hospitals, physician groups, clinics HCA, CommonSpirit, Kaiser, CVS MinuteClinic Fee-for-service (paid per procedure) or value-based care (paid for outcomes)
PBMs Middlemen negotiating drug prices CVS Caremark, Express Scripts, OptumRx Rebates from pharma, spread pricing, admin fees
Medtech & Diagnostics Devices, equipment, lab testing Medtronic, Abbott, Danaher, Intuitive Surgical, Illumina Sell to hospitals/providers; some direct-to-consumer

Key dynamics shaping the industry

Dynamic What's happening Why it matters
Vertical integration Payers buying providers (UHC → Optum), pharmacies buying payers (CVS → Aetna) Blurring lines; harder to analyze as pure-plays
GLP-1 drugs Ozempic/Wegovy (Novo), Mounjaro/Zepbound (Lilly) redefining obesity treatment $100B+ market by 2030; ripple effects on bariatric surgery, food companies, payers
Value-based care shift CMS pushing providers from fee-for-service to outcomes-based payment Changes incentives—prevention becomes profitable
Patent cliffs Major drugs losing exclusivity (Humira, Keytruda coming) Biosimilar competition, M&A to fill pipeline gaps
AI in drug discovery Startups (Recursion, Insitro) + big pharma bets Could compress timelines, but clinical trials still slow
Site-of-care shift Care moving from hospitals to outpatient, ambulatory, home Margin pressure on hospitals; growth in ASCs, home health

Regulatory landscape

Entity What they control Current posture
FDA Drug/device approval Accelerated approvals under scrutiny post-Aduhelm controversy
CMS Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement rates Pushing value-based care; drug price negotiation starting (IRA)
FTC Antitrust on M&A, PBM practices Aggressive under Khan; blocking hospital mergers, suing PBMs
State AGs Drug pricing, surprise billing Varies by state; growing activism

Jargon cheat sheet

PBM Pharmacy Benefit Manager—middlemen between pharma and payers
Formulary List of drugs an insurer will cover
Rebate Discount pharma pays PBM to get on formulary (opaque, controversial)
ASC Ambulatory Surgery Center—outpatient surgery facility
340B Federal program giving discounted drugs to safety-net providers
IRA Inflation Reduction Act—allows Medicare to negotiate drug prices (starting 2026)
Biosimilar Generic-equivalent for biologic drugs
NDA/BLA New Drug Application / Biologics License Application—FDA approval filings
PMPM Per Member Per Month—how payers measure costs

GLP-1 Timeline: How We Got Here

2005
FDA approves Byetta (exenatide)
First GLP-1 for diabetes. Proof of concept for GLP-1 mechanism.
2014
FDA approves Saxenda (liraglutide) for obesity
First GLP-1 with obesity indication. Modest weight loss (~5-7%).
2017
FDA approves Ozempic (semaglutide)
Novo Nordisk's blockbuster begins its journey.
JUN 2021
FDA approves Wegovy for obesity
Same drug, higher dose—massive demand begins.[2]
2022
Wegovy shortages begin; TikTok/celebrity buzz explodes
Supply-demand imbalance; payers start restricting coverage.
MAY 2022
Lilly announces tirzepatide (Mounjaro) Phase 3 data
Dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist shows superior efficacy (up to 22.5% weight loss).
2023
Mounjaro approved for diabetes; off-label obesity use surges
Lilly enters the race. Doctors prescribe off-label for weight loss.
NOV 2023
Zepbound (tirzepatide) approved for obesity
Lilly's dual-agonist enters as direct Wegovy competitor.
2024
Compounding pharmacy controversy; FDA crackdowns
Gray market expands as branded supply constrained. Safety concerns emerge.
2025
Novo and Lilly both hit manufacturing capacity issues
Neither can meet demand; oral GLP-1 pipelines accelerate.
FEB 2026
High-dose Zepbound approved
26% average weight loss. Efficacy bar raised again.[3]
